# Safety Notes for Audio Integration

## Electrical Safety

- Do not connect speaker outputs directly to line-level inputs.
- Verify expected voltage levels before connecting any analyzer input.
- Use resistor attenuators or commercial speaker-to-line converters for speaker-level capture.

## Equipment Protection

- Start volume low, then increase slowly.
- Keep headroom (3-6 dB) to avoid clipping and potential downstream distortion.
- Avoid connecting multiple amplifiers to the same passive speaker load.

## Grounding and Noise

- Keep AVR, analyzer, and USB interface on a common power strip when possible.
- Use short shielded RCA cables.
- Add line isolation transformer if hum persists.

## Operational Safety Checklist

1. Confirm source type (line-level vs speaker-level).
2. Confirm correct wiring path.
3. Confirm attenuation hardware when required.
4. Power on with low volume.
5. Verify levels and clipping behavior before long measurements.
